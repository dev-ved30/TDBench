Transportation

Getting to Chicago

By Air

O'Hare International Airport (ORD) is the primary airport serving Chicago, located approximately 17 miles from downtown. Midway International Airport (MDW) is a closer alternative at about 10 miles from downtown.

By Train

Chicago Union Station is served by Amtrak with connections from across the country. From Union Station, take the CTA Red Line north to Chicago/State station, then walk or take a short taxi ride to Michigan Avenue.

By Car

Chicago is accessible via I-90/94 (Kennedy/Dan Ryan Expressway) from the north and south, and I-290 (Eisenhower Expressway) from the west. Valet parking is available at The Westin for an additional fee.

Airport to Hotel

From O'Hare: Take the CTA Blue Line to Clark/Lake, transfer to the Red Line northbound to Chicago/State. The hotel is a short walk east on Chicago Ave. Alternatively, taxis and rideshare are available (~$40-50).

About Chicago

Make the most of your visit to the Windy City

Dining

Chicago is renowned for its diverse culinary scene. From deep-dish pizza to Michelin-starred restaurants, you'll find excellent dining options within walking distance of the hotel on Michigan Avenue and the surrounding neighborhoods.

Attractions

Visit world-class museums including the Art Institute of Chicago, Field Museum, and Adler Planetarium. Take an architecture river cruise, explore Millennium Park, or enjoy views from the 360 Chicago observation deck (in the Hancock Center, right across the street!).

Weather

Early March in Chicago can be variable. Expect temperatures between 30-45°F (-1 to 7°C). We recommend bringing layers and a warm jacket. Rain and occasional snow are possible, so pack accordingly.
