Our Purpose

Vision

Advancing Transient Science

TDAbench 2026 brings together astronomers, data scientists, and machine learning experts to create a comprehensive benchmark dataset that will accelerate the classification of astronomical transients in the era of large-scale surveys.

Community Collaboration

By fostering open collaboration between domain experts and ML practitioners, we aim to establish standardized evaluation metrics and best practices that will benefit the entire astronomical community.

Open Science

All datasets, tools, and methodologies developed during TDAbench 2026 will be made freely available to the community, ensuring broad impact and reproducibility in transient classification research.

What We Aim to Achieve

Goals

Our workshop is structured around five key objectives that will guide our collaborative efforts

01

Define Classification Taxonomy

Establish a comprehensive and scientifically rigorous taxonomy for astronomical transient classification that serves as the foundation for benchmark development.

02

Curate High-Quality Data

Compile and validate a diverse dataset of well-characterized transients with multi-wavelength observations and spectroscopic confirmations.

03

Standardize Evaluation Metrics

Develop and agree upon standardized metrics and evaluation protocols for comparing machine learning classification methods.

04

Identify Key Challenges

Document the main challenges in transient classification and establish research priorities for the community to address.

05

Build Community Infrastructure

Create sustainable infrastructure for dataset hosting, model evaluation, and community engagement beyond the workshop.

06

Foster Long-term Collaboration

Establish working groups and communication channels to continue development and maintenance of the benchmark dataset.

The Benchmark

Data

Building a Comprehensive Benchmark Dataset

The TDAbench dataset will serve as the definitive benchmark for evaluating machine learning approaches to astronomical transient classification. Our goal is to create a dataset that is:

  • Representative of the full diversity of transient phenomena
  • Well-characterized with high-confidence labels
  • Multi-wavelength and multi-epoch coverage
  • Balanced to address class imbalance challenges
  • Documented with comprehensive metadata
  • Designed for realistic evaluation scenarios

The dataset will include photometric light curves, host galaxy properties, contextual information, and where available, spectroscopic classifications from major transient surveys.
