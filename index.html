Vision

TDAbench 2026 brings together astronomers, data scientists, and machine learning experts to address one of the most pressing challenges in time-domain astronomy: the development of a comprehensive, standardized benchmark dataset for the classification of astronomical transients.

As surveys like the Vera C. Rubin Observatory's Legacy Survey of Space and Time (LSST) prepare to discover millions of transient events nightly, the need for robust, well-characterized training data becomes paramount. Our workshop aims to establish community standards and create resources that will accelerate progress in automated transient classification.

Goals

Define Standards

Establish community-wide standards for transient classification taxonomy, data formats, and evaluation metrics that enable fair comparison of different ML approaches.

Curate Data

Identify, collect, and curate high-quality labeled transient data from multiple surveys and sources to create a comprehensive training dataset.

Build Infrastructure

Develop the technical infrastructure needed to host, distribute, and maintain the benchmark dataset for long-term community use.

Foster Collaboration

Create lasting collaborations between survey teams, ML researchers, and domain experts to ensure the benchmark remains relevant and up-to-date.

Data

The TDAbench dataset will encompass a diverse range of astronomical transients including:

  • Supernovae (Type Ia, II, Ib/c, and subtypes)
  • Tidal Disruption Events (TDEs)
  • Active Galactic Nuclei (AGN) variability
  • Kilonovae and gravitational wave counterparts
  • Stellar flares and variable stars
  • Fast Radio Bursts (FRB) optical counterparts
  • And many more transient classes...

Data will be drawn from major surveys including ZTF, ATLAS, Pan-STARRS, and archival sources, with careful attention to selection effects, completeness, and label quality.
